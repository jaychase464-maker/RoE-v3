## Installation

1. Back up or commit the current project state.
2. Copy the package into the `RoE v3` project root and replace matching files.
3. Allow Unity to finish compiling.
4. Exit Play Mode.
5. Run `Rules of Entry > Milestone 6C > Build Deployment and In-Mission Tablet`.
6. Run `Tools > Rules of Entry > Milestone 6C > Validate Deployment and In-Mission Tablet`.
7. Run all EditMode tests.
8. Enter headquarters through Operations, select an entry and officers, deploy, then test the operation.

Milestone 6C must be rerun after rebuilding Milestone 4 officer prefabs or regenerating the operation scene.

## Controls inside an operation

- `Tab`: raise or lower the operational tablet.
- `1`: Situation page while the tablet is open.
- `2`: Objectives page while the tablet is open.
- `3`: Body Cameras page while the tablet is open.
- `Q` / `E` or left/right arrows: previous/next deployed officer feed.
- Gamepad shoulders: previous/next deployed officer feed.
- `Escape` or gamepad East/B: close the tablet.
- The buttons are also selectable with the mouse or Input System UI navigation.

## Expected behavior

- Headquarters entry selection resolves to one of three authored stable-ID scene anchors.
- The player and assigned officers begin at the selected entry.
- Officers removed from the headquarters team do not deploy and do not appear in the Tactical HUD or body-camera list.
- The operation continues in real time while the tablet is open; opening it disables player movement, looking, weapons, interaction, and squad commands without pausing AI.
- The Body Cameras page opens by default.
- Only the selected officer camera renders. Switching feeds disables the previous camera and reuses the same RenderTexture.
- Body-camera metadata shows officer identity, camera ID, mission timestamp, recording state, signal, qualitative condition, qualitative ammunition, and current order.
- The tablet never exposes exact rounds, hit points, hidden suspect state, or score internals.
- Direct Training entry without headquarters deployment keeps the existing prototype spawn and exposes all configured scene officers.

## Common problems

- Tablet does not appear on `Tab`: rerun Milestone 6C setup outside Play Mode and confirm the validator finds `[ROE_InMissionTablet]`.
- Feed is black: confirm the selected officer is active, `OfficerBodyCameraSource` reports a signal, and HDRP created additional camera data on `ROE_OfficerBodyCamera`.
- Entry deployment fails NavMesh placement: the prototype NavMesh or greybox changed after the anchors were authored; reposition the anchor spawn transforms on baked NavMesh and rerun validation.
- Removed officer still appears: confirm deployment began through headquarters and `OperationDeploymentContext` contains the expected officer IDs.
- Body-camera feed is upside down or obstructed after final character integration: reauthor the body-camera mount transform on the final chest/vest attachment point.
- Rebuilding Milestone 4 removes cameras: expected because Milestone 4 recreates officer prefabs; rerun Milestone 6C last.

## Known boundaries

- Current body-camera cameras use temporary root-relative chest mounts because final officer rigs and armor do not exist.
- Signal is explicit but does not yet simulate RF occlusion, range, interference, latency, dropped frames, battery drain, or evidence retention.
- The screen-space tablet has no first-person hands, physical model, raise/lower animation, or animation-driven obstruction.
- Entry anchors belong to the current prototype greybox and must be reauthored for the first real mission map.
- Final body-camera devices, uniform mounting, tablet model, first-person arms, and animations are presentation work after gameplay validation.
